# Guardians Guide

[Version française](GUIDE_GARDIENS_FR.md)

A Guardian organizes the Kingdom and protects the game’s balance. The role is not about monitoring other members. It is about making responsibilities clear, effort visible, and progress motivating.

A Guardian may also play, complete missions, and develop their own hero.

## Guardian responsibilities

A Guardian can act in five main areas:

1. members and access;
2. quests and validation;
3. rewards and Bosses;
4. Kingdom resources and construction;
5. collective activity and follow-up.

## 1. Managing members

From **Kingdom Members**, an active Guardian can:

- invite new members;
- choose an Adventurer, Mercenary, or Guardian role according to their permissions;
- promote a member to Guardian or change them back to Adventurer;
- deactivate access;
- request a password reset for a member;
- view each person’s level, XP, gold, role, and avatar.

The founding owner has special protections. In particular, only the owner can authorize another Guardian invitation, and Guardians do not manage their own role from the member card.

For a Mercenary, choose:

- a temporary duration from 1 to 30 days;
- access to the whole Kingdom or only one Domain.

Use Domain access for a guest who should only see and select missions from one part of the household.

## 2. Creating and organizing quests

From the **Grand Quest Register**, a Guardian can create, edit, assign, and archive quests.

For every quest, review:

- its title, description, icon, and Domain;
- difficulty;
- frequency: **one time**, **daily**, or **weekly**;
- XP, gold, skill, and Boss-damage gains;
- whether manual validation is required;
- assigned members;
- the availability date and time if it should not be taken immediately.

A scheduled quest is visible but cannot be taken before its planned time. The app displays its availability date.

Daily and weekly assignments are preserved. Each participant can complete the quest once in their own period, then receives it automatically on the next day or week. You do not need to reassign it.

A member may leave a recurring assignment unless a completion is waiting for validation. Their history remains intact.

## 3. Validating with care

The **Validations** page contains completions that need your approval. You can:

- **approve** the completion and distribute its gains;
- select **Try again** and clearly explain what is missing.

A short, encouraging explanation is better than a penalty. Describe the expected result, not the person.

Some quests can be configured without manual validation. Approving your own completion is also supported when the Kingdom’s organization requires it, especially in a household with only one Guardian.

## 4. Reviewing initiatives

From level 10, Adventurers and Mercenaries can suggest initiatives. Guardians review them under **Initiatives to review**.

Before accepting one, check:

- that the task is useful and achievable;
- that the Domain is correct;
- that difficulty and gains are proportionate;
- that a Mercenary’s scope is respected.

An accepted initiative becomes a mission assigned to its author. An unsuitable proposal may be declined.

## 5. Organizing collective rewards

Adventurers and Mercenaries may submit reward wishes. Guardians remain the decision-makers and can:

- accept or decline a wish;
- define its final title and description;
- set a target number of quests;
- attach it to a Boss;
- directly create an official reward;
- edit, deliver, or archive a reward.

Order accepted rewards with the priority controls. **Priority 1** receives progress from the next approved quest. Once its target is reached, later quests automatically switch to the next reward.

Only confirm **Reward delivered** after it has actually been given. It then joins the Chronicles. Abandoned rewards can be archived and remain available in the archived section.

## 6. Managing Bosses

A Boss is a collective objective. Every approved quest deals its planned damage.

A Guardian can:

- summon a Boss and define its characteristics;
- follow its health and planned rewards;
- replace or retire it when necessary;
- attach a Boss to a collective reward.

A victory may grant XP, skill progress, crystals, and special items. Avoid making a Boss too long or too easy: it should remain motivating for the group.

## 7. Developing the Kingdom

Kingdom progression uses two complementary systems:

- **evolution buildings**, unlocked by the number of completed quests;
- **reserve-funded constructions**, paid for with wood, stone, provisions, crystals, or Boss items.

Quests mainly earn wood, stone, and provisions. Defeated Bosses may grant crystals and special items.

Only Guardians can commit collective reserves to:

- start or upgrade a construction;
- convert crystals into resources;
- choose the Kingdom’s investment order.

Before a major expense, explain the goal to the group. Resources are earned collectively and should support progress that everyone can understand.

## 8. Following Kingdom activity

Remember to check:

- **notifications**, especially for new assignments or initiatives;
- the **Hall of Heroes**, to see progress without turning the game into a competition;
- the **Kingdom Legend** and **Chronicles**, which preserve important milestones;
- **Supplies**, shared by Guardians, Adventurers, and Mercenaries.

If you manage several Kingdoms, always confirm which one is active before making a change.

## Suggested routine

### Every day

- review pending validations;
- answer new initiatives;
- check that no urgent mission is blocked;
- encourage progress instead of only pointing out delays.

### Every week

- review the balance between quests and gains;
- adjust reward priorities;
- check the Boss and Kingdom reserves;
- archive content that is no longer useful;
- ask the group what remains motivating and what should change.

## Balancing guidelines

- A short task should grant less than a long or difficult one.
- Boss damage should reflect the effort required.
- Daily quests should remain realistic and limited in number.
- A weekly quest must return once per week, not every day.
- Rewards should be achievable and suitable for the household.
- The Guardian role is for arbitration, never humiliation or pressure.

## Quick questions

**Must I reassign daily and weekly quests?**

No. Assignments are preserved and availability returns automatically in each new period.

**Why can’t a member take a quest?**

Check its availability date, completion for the current period, any pending validation, and a Mercenary’s authorized Domain.

**Who can spend Kingdom resources?**

Guardians only.

**What happens to an archived reward?**

It no longer advances in the active queue but remains visible under **Archived Rewards**.
